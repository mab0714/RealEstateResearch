This research paper drills down into the **Charlotte, NC metropolitan area** (Mecklenburg County) to perform a micro-level neighborhood analysis across **8 representative ZIP codes**. By evaluating localized home values, rents, school quality, poverty rates, and price volatility, we rank these submarkets using the **Real Estate Opportunity Index (REOI)**.

3. **East Charlotte (28215 - REOI: 33.2)** and **Northwest Charlotte (28216 - REOI: 25.7)** offer excellent cash flow yields ($3.0\%–3.2\%$) but carry significantly higher socioeconomic and tenant risks, including elevated poverty rates ($14\%–16\%$) and lower school quality indicators.
4. **NoDa / Plaza Midwood (28205 - REOI: 25.2)** ranks last in our cohort. High home values ($468k) relative to standard average rents ($1,800/mo) result in a compressed net yield ($1.95\%$) and a lower overall rentability profile.

## Step-by-Step Calculation Walkthrough: Steele Creek (28273)

To illustrate the mathematical scoring, here is the exact derivation for **Steele Creek (28273)**.

### 1. Net Yield Calculation
- **Home Value ($H$):** $372,913 | **Monthly Rent ($R_{\text{mo}}$):** $2,050 | **Property Tax:** $0.95\% | **Income Tax:** $4.5\%$
- **Gross Rent:** $\$2,050 \times 12 = \$24,600$
- **Operating Expenses (35%):** $\$24,600 \times 0.35 = \$8,610$
- **Annual Property Taxes:** $\$372,913 \times 0.0095 = \$3,542.67$
- **NOI (before income tax):** $\$24,600 - \$8,610 - \$3,542.67 = \$12,447.33$
- **State Income Tax Drag (4.5%):** $\$12,447.33 \times 0.045 = \$560.13$
- **Net Annual Cash Flow:** $\$12,447.33 - \$560.13 = \$11,887.20$
- **Net Yield ($Y_{\text{net}}$):**
  $$Y_{\text{net}}$ = \frac{\$11,887.20}{\$372,913} \times 100 = 3.187\% \approx 3.19\%$$
